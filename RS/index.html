Oil Prices hit historic low

Oil prices goes down week after week. But what does it mean for us - ordinary people? Earlier this year, crude oil price fell below $20 for the first time in almost 20 years. Usually, a drop-in oil prices meant a whole breaking point to the companies in spheres that have to pay for oil. But what’s more important is that regular consumers, Ordinary people like us also benefit from low oil prices, as this results in cheap gas, cheaper airplane tickets, etc. Airlines, one of the largest beneficiaries of low fuel prices, have stopped 30% of the flights as people around the world are Staying home to prevent the spread of the coronavirus. According to some analytics, airlines are likely to “save” more than $9 billion under one scenario. Still, it is a controversial data as there is no one actually flying, so there is no profit as well. Here is a problematic situation as the price of oil lower than the cost of production.
